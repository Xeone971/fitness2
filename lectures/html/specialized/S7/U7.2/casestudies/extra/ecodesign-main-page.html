Introduction

Ecodesign consists in taking into account the environmental footprint of products and services in the general desing process. The overall goal is the decrease of the environmental impact as compared to a reference state and to avoid shifting environmental burdens from a specific process or product to another.

Ecodesign in the packaging sector includes a several number of different methodologies, which will be presented here. Because of the very integrated character of packaging in the supply chain of goods much care has to be taken to avoid the shifting burdens issue.Key notions and principles of ecodesing are presented in this course.

To better understand this course, some pre-requisites are necessary. They can be found in the following Fitness courses:

  • Properties of food packaging materials: It is essential to have a strong knowledge of the physical and chemical caracteristics a packaging has or must have to fulfill its function.
  • Environmental impact of packaging and recycling: Main environmental impacts of packaging are presented in this course.
  • Methodologies used in life cycle assessment: Life cycle analysis and assesment (LCA) allows the quantification of environmental impacts.
  • Food packaging and shelf life: The main function of packaging is to protect food, the shelf-life is an essential element to take into account when ecodesigning a product.

Organization of the course

This course is organized in the following way: a main presentation on Ecodesign will allow you to understand the key elements to ecodesign. A step-by-step animation then presents the key points for the ecodesing of a packaging system throughout its life cycle. The toolbox gathers a number of ecodesing tools which can be applied at different stages of the design process. For one central overview, you will be able to navigate to get further information on a given item. To illustrate key notions of ecodesign, environmental comparisons of different packagings are presented using LCA methodology. To better understand the nature of LCA, a presentation of some key notions are given.

Key points of ecodesign using a step-by-step approach

Ecodesign should always be done having a holistic point of view, and the reduction of the environmental burden in one step should not increase the burden to another step. This presentation guides you step-by-step through the issues which can be encountered in packaging ecodesign.
