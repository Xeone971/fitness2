### What is Ecodesign?

### Ecodesign of products

* Design approach with consideration for the environmental impacts of the product during its **whole lifecycle**.
* Objective to reduce ecological footprint of a product **at equal functionality or equal service delivered.**

**The goal is to have a holistic, integrative point of view to assess design options in the light of associated ecological impacts**.

Ecodesign is a *multi-criteria*and *multi-step*analysis.

It is a change from **curative** to **preventive** approaches.

Source: Ventère, 2012, Techniques de l’Ingénieur, [G 6000]

---

### Why ecodesign?

* Protection of the environment

* Innovative advantage

* Compliance with and anticipation of legislation

* Growing market demand of environmentally friendly products

* Enhance the enterprise's image

* Redesign of industrial processes, reduce manufacturing and logistics costs

* Detection of new opportunities for value creation

* Set apart from the competitors

* Develop new markets of higher added value

* Stimulate innovation…

---

### Environmental impacts

1. There are different types of environmental impacts: climate change, depletion of natural resources, greenhouse effect, water pollution, air pollution, soil pollution, etc.

2. The environmental impacts of a product can be generated during the different stages of the life cycle: production, transport, distribution, use, end-of-life.

3. There is no "zero impact" product: any product needs raw materials and energy, must be packaged and transported, and will generate some waste.

Nowadays the most common approach to evaluating environmental impacts is the [**LCA approach**](https://fitness.agroparistech.fr/fitness/lectures/html/specialized/S7/U7.1/part1.html#/8).

### Linear economy concept: Down-Cycling

![](images/downcycling.jpg)





A large part of plastic packagings recycling processes do not recylce the waste back to materials which are [food-grade](). Recyling from one application into the same application is called closed-loop recycling. If the material is used instead for another purpose, one speaks about open-loop recycling. Most of the times the material is **downcycled** towards another, less technical, application or product (ex: waste bags from recycled films). Therefore, the production of packaging by the food industry generally continues to require the extraction of new fossil raw material.

However **some** plastics such as PET, can be recycled in close-loop process back to PET bottles. 

---

### Linear economy concept: Energy Recovery

![energy recovery](images/energy-recovery.jpg)

For the plastics that are not recycled, incineration is the common end-of-life treatment. Nowadays, technology allows **energy recovery** from the incineration process. However, this energy recovery does not resolve the problem of the **extraction of fossil fuel ressources**.

---

### Circular economy concept

To resolve the problems related to the depletion of fossil ressources, a transition towards a **circular economy** is neccessary, in which products are collected after use and recycled to the product manufacturing process, using the hierachy: repair - reuse - remanufacture - recylce.  

![circular](images/circular-economy-concept.jpg)

Adapted from Learningfactory Ecodesing Manual, Fraunhofer IZM, 2018 

The European commsion has established goals to get to towards circular economy in its [Circular Economy Action Plan](https://ec.europa.eu/environment/circular-economy/index_en.htm) .

### Innovative design

Unlike redesign (eco-design), innovative design implies a **paradigm shift** (Cooper model) that requires inventing **new systems or products**. 

To achieve this it is necessary:

1. To redefine what one seeks to create as a service, a function ... This requires exploring the expectations of the needs of the people (customers but not only producers, processors ...) who will be affected by this innovation.

2. To move from a **knowledge exploitation mode** (*I mobilize the knowledge that I have or that my group has*) to a mode of **exploration of knowledge** (*to go in search of knowledge*):
   * that exists but which I do not have and therefore I do not think to mobilize
   * that needs to be created to achieve innovation. 

For this exploration the **C-K concept** can be useful.

### C-K design theory

![C-K theory](images/c-k-theory.jpg)

* **Concept space**: Product proposition can be widened by **acquired knowledge**.
* **Knowledge space**: knowledge acquisition is driven by **novel product propositions**.

### Design process – Creative problem solving

<img src="images/design-process.jpg" style="zoom:100%;" />



**At each step:**

Divergent and convergent thinking

1. Collect all individual ideas
2. Elaborate collective synthesis and idea selection

**Never mix up task 1 and 2 ! They are strictly separated in time.**

----

### Principles of creative problem solving

* **Ask problems as questions.** Open-ended questions generate more answers, problem statements tend to generate limited responses.

* **Defer or suspend judgment in divergent phases.** Judging solutions early on tends to shut down the idea generation. In convergent phases time is given to evaluate ideas. 

* **Focus on "Yes, and," rather than "No, but."** "Yes, and" encourages to expand thoughts.  "But" – even if preceded by "yes" ends conversation and can negate previous ideas. 

**Balance divergent and convergent thinking and separate those processes**.

Here are some **Brainstorming techniques**, which are meant to change the context of the participants to free idea generation:

* Inspiration by images

* Changing context: In the future, in a space capsule, …. 

* Writing of constraint texts

Source: INRAE – Formations on creative problem solving – L. Prevosto. From the theory of Alex Osborne - Applied Imagination: Principles and Procedures of Creative Problem Solving. New York, NY: Charles Scribner’s Sons, 1953

### Consumer’s perception of eco-design

Attributes common to LCA practitioners and consumer perspective: 

* Packaging from **recyclable materials or environmentally friendly** materials

* **Smaller weight** (**cardboard, paper, recycled plastic, and biodegradable materials**)

* **Optimized content/container ratio**

* **Packaging color:** green, blue, or transparent, shape round, curved, delicate 

* **Eco-label** or other environmental label
