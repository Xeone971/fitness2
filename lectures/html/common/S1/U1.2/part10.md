# Wood properties
![Slide10.svg](./src_part10/Slide10.svg  "slide 10 of 19") 

note: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/action/showCitFormats?doi=10.1111%2F1541-4337.12199

### Wood properties
* Wood has favorable mechanical, physical, and natural chemical properties for a packaging material Resistance to compressive and tensile forces, flexural strength, impact and splitting ability and hardness Low thermal and electrical conductivity Large hydration capacity Wood can take up a considerable amount of water and release it again, depending on the relative humidity of the environment The uptake of water causes a swelling of the wood structure due to water adsorption, while the removal of water leads to shrinking Typical sorption isotherm values Relative Humidity Water content 30% 7% 60% 10% 80% 14%
* Source: Adapted from - Transport Information Service, 2015 http://www.tis-gdv.de

# Regulation and safety aspects
![Slide12.svg](./src_part10/Slide12.svg  "slide 12 of 19") 

note: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/action/showCitFormats?doi=10.1111%2F1541-4337.12199

### Regulation and safety aspects
* Phytosanitary measures Directive 2005/15/EC sets the requirements for non-EU imports into the EU of wood packaging material: All wood packaging material from non-EU countries must be: either heat treated or fumigated in line with ISPM15 procedures; officially marked with the ISPM15 stamp consisting of 3 codes (country, producer and measure applied) and the IPPC logo; debarked - These requirements do not apply to: wood 6 mm thick or less; material made entirely from processed wood produced using glue, heat and pressure e.g. plywood, oriented strand board and veneer; material used in trade within the EU
